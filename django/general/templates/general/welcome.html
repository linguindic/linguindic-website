Welcome

Welcome to the LINGUINDIC project website. LINGUINDIC is an EU-funded research project led by Dr John Lowe at the University of Oxford in the UK. It aims to systematically explore and analyse the neglected riches of ancient Indian linguistic thought. It will also uncover lost linguistic insights and analyses, as well as build on these insights to create innovative approaches to contemporary issues in modern Western linguistics. Overall, the project will focus on ancient Indian contributions to linguistic thought in three broad areas: morphosyntax and formal language systems, semantics/pragmatics and the philosophy of language, and phonetics/phonology.

You can engage with the project through this website by:

  • Reading detailed information about the project, our team, related events, and more
  • Browsing our database of linguistic notions, linguistic fields, authors, texts, and references
  • Comparing two linguistic notions from our database
  • Visualising our data, to identify patterns and trends and thus gain a better understand database

If you'd like to get in touch with us about the project or this website, you can email us at linguindic@orinst.ox.ac.uk
